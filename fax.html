HIPAA & Privacy

Harbor Privacy Fax operates under the HIPAA conduit exception (45 CFR §164.500(b)). That is the same legal category as a telephone company or postal carrier. We transmit your fax and then it is gone. We do not open it, store it, or retain any copy.

What that means practically: if you are sending medical records, insurance forms, or other health information, no Business Associate Agreement is required because we never hold the data. It passes through and is deleted on delivery.

This is not a subscription fax service. There is no account, no inbox, and no record of your transaction stored on our servers. It is a one-time send, built for people who need to fax something sensitive without leaving a trail.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an account to send a fax?

No. Harbor Privacy Fax requires no account, no login, and no personal information. Just upload your document, enter the fax number, and pay.

What happens to my document after it's sent?

Your document is permanently and irreversibly deleted the moment delivery is confirmed. Nothing is stored on Harbor Privacy servers — not your file, not the fax number, not the cover page.

Is this HIPAA compliant?

Yes. Harbor Privacy Fax qualifies under the HIPAA conduit exception (45 CFR §164.500(b)) -- the same rule that covers telephone companies and postal carriers. We transmit your fax without storing or accessing it. No Business Associate Agreement is required. Your document is deleted the moment delivery is confirmed.

How much does it cost?

$2.99 per fax for up to 10 pages. Need to send more? Add the extra pages option (+$1.99) to send up to 30 pages. An optional unbranded cover page is available for an additional $0.99.

What file types can I send?

PDF, JPG, PNG, and DOCX are all supported. Maximum 10 pages and 20MB per fax.

Can I send a fax from my phone?

Yes. It works from any phone or computer — no app download required. You can also install it as a web app from your browser for quick home screen access.

Can I send medical records or legal documents?

Yes. Harbor Privacy Fax is regularly used for medical record requests, insurance forms, legal filings, and other sensitive documents. No account means nothing to link back to you, and your document is gone the moment it arrives.
